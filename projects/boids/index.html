Overview

This is a simulation showing the basic flocking behaviour of boids. The movement of each agent is governed by three basic rules: separation, alignment and cohesion. Separation doesn't allow a boid to get too close to another one, alignment sets the velocity of a boid to be the average velocity of its neighbours and cohesion keeps a boid from straying too far from a local group. Although the rules are only imposed on individual boids, complex leaderless group behaviour emerges. Additional rules can be added, for example avoiding predators or targeting food. The graphics and camera movement are done using three.js.
