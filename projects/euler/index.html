Computational Fluid Dynamics with Embedded Cut Cells on Graphics Hardware

Density pseudocolour of a 100 m/s flow around the DrivAer geometry

As part of my degree I research speeding up fluid simulations on graphics hardware. The focus is on reducing the runtimes of CFD simulations with embedded cut cell geometries using the CUDA programming platform. The resulting software pipeline uses the Euler equations to model high speed flow and shock waves in domains with static rigid obstacles. These scenarios are applicable to anything from automotive and aerospace research to insurance assessment and mining safety.

The software uses a dimensionally-split sweeping algorithm to find the propagation of information in each direction of a regular Cartesian domain. The produced code has been validated against several exact and experimental results to show the correctness and accuracy of the software package. We can robustly handle near vacuum scenarios, capture shock diffraction and reflection and high speed vortices.

As the memory on a GPU is relatively limited, we also look at the performance and scaling of multi-card scenarios using MPI for high resolution simulations.
